I’m a Master’s student in Computer Engineering at NYU Tandon, advised by Chinmay Hegde. My research focuses on scalable oversight of language models, specifically adversarial methods for hardening rubric-based judges in non-verifiable domains.

Before NYU I studied Computer Science and Managerial Economics at UMass Amherst. I’m broadly interested in alignment, evaluation, and the failure modes of learned reward signals.

Research

Adversarial Rubric Optimization for LLM Alignment
M.S. Thesis · in progress, 2026
A two-phase iterative loop: a GRPO-trained exploit model surfaces preference transitivity violations in rubric-conditioned judges, and a rubric generator is fine-tuned to harden against those exploits. Built on Llama-3.3-70B (judge) and RubricARM-8B (generator). The framing grew out of earlier work characterizing multi-dimensional preference landscapes in DPO, where I found high cross-factor correlation and low discriminatory power of harmlessness across 12 factors and 5 models. Those results pointed at evaluation as the underlying bottleneck and motivated the shift toward attacking judge robustness directly. research overview
Fine-Tuning LLMs Without Normalization: DyT under SFT
NYU ECE-GY 9143 (HPML) · 2025
Extended Dynamic Tanh (Zhu et al., CVPR 2025) from pretraining into post-training. Replaced LayerNorm in DistilGPT2 and Pythia-410M with DyT, then evaluated across two tracks (PEFT with LoRA and full SFT) on Alpaca, ShareGPT, and RE-WILD. DyT’s pretraining gains did not transfer cleanly to either regime. The loss gap shrank with scale, surfacing a relationship between model size and DyT’s training stability under fine-tuning. code & report
